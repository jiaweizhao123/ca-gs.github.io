Abstract

Recently, 3D Gaussian Splatting (3DGS) has demonstrated remarkable advancements in novel view synthesis, achieving exceptional rendering quality with high efficiency. However, its significant dependence on the initial point cloud often results in artifacts in areas characterized by sparse data. A primary contributor to this issue is the inadequate growth condition during the densification process. Specifically, relying solely on the average position gradient across multiple views distorts the growth requirements for individual perspectives. We identify that occlusion issues between viewpoints can lead to Gaussians accumulating gradients that vary significantly or even result in zero gradients. To mitigate this problem, we propose a contribution-weighted gradient accumulation method that prioritizes high-contribution views to derive a more accurate average gradient. Furthermore, we introduce a straightforward floater removal algorithm to eliminate many floaters based on camera distance and relative depth differences. Extensive experiments validate that our approach reduces blur artifacts and floaters while achieving superior rendering quality. On mainstream datasets, our method either outperforms or matches state-of-the-art performance.

Method

Motivation of CA-GS. Schematic of occlusion between different viewpoints, where the occlusion level of Gaussians is assessed from the red viewpoint. (a) A completely occluded Gaussian does not participate in the red viewpoint's alpha blending process, accumulating zero positional gradients. In contrast, a partially occluded Gaussian participates to varying degrees in the rendering images of the red and green viewpoints, resulting in significantly different accumulated gradients. (b) Taking as an example the two highlighted Gaussians in blue, occlusion issues can adversely affect positional gradient averaging results and hinder the growth of Gaussians.
